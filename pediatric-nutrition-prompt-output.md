# 7-Day Feeding Schedule for a 9-Month-Old Baby (South Indian Vegetarian)

This guide helps parents transition a **9‑month‑old baby from milk to
solid foods** using a **South Indian vegetarian diet (no eggs)**. The
plan gradually increases solids while keeping milk feeds for comfort and
nutrition.

# Transition Strategy

At 9 months, milk remains important but solids should gradually
increase.

**Week 1--2** - Milk: 4--5 feeds per day - Small portions of solids

**Week 3--4** - Milk: 3--4 feeds - Larger solid meals

**After 10--11 months** - Milk: \~3 feeds - Most calories from solids

Tip: Offer **solid foods before milk during daytime meals**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Safe Foods for 9‑Month Babies

**Grains** - Rice - Ragi - Idli - Dosa - Broken wheat

**Proteins** - Moong dal - Toor dal - Masoor dal - Homemade curd

**Vegetables** - Carrot - Pumpkin - Sweet potato - Beans - Spinach

**Fruits** - Banana - Papaya - Apple (steamed) - Pear

**Healthy fats** - Small amounts of ghee

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Foods to Avoid

Avoid these under 1 year:

-   Honey
-   Whole nuts
-   Raw vegetables
-   Added salt
-   Added sugar
-   Processed foods
-   Packaged snacks
-   Cow's milk as a main drink

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Portion Guidelines

  Food Type    Portion
  ------------ ------------------
  Main meals   3--5 tablespoons
  Fruits       2--3 tablespoons
  Snacks       2--3 tablespoons
  Milk feeds   As needed

Never force feed. Baby appetite varies daily.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Signs Baby Is Ready for More Solids

-   Finishes meals quickly
-   Shows interest in family food
-   Opens mouth when spoon approaches
-   Chews soft lumps
-   Drinks small sips of water

These signs indicate baby can increase **portion size or texture**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Hydration Guidance

Offer **small sips of boiled and cooled water** after meals.

Typical intake:

**60--120 ml per day**

Use a **cup or spoon instead of a bottle**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Simple Homemade Recipes

## Vegetable Khichdi

Cook rice, moong dal, carrot, and pumpkin with extra water.\
Mash well and add a drop of ghee.

## Ragi Porridge

Cook ragi flour slowly in water while stirring continuously until
smooth.

## Soft Pongal

Cook rice and moong dal until very soft and mash well.

## Steamed Apple Mash

Steam apple pieces for 5 minutes and mash until smooth.

## Banana Curd Mix

Mash ripe banana and mix with one spoon fresh curd.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Parent Tip:** Introduce **one new food every 2--3 days** and monitor
for allergies or digestion issues.
